Sentry: Smart Stormwater Valve Innovation

Mitigating Climate-Driven Flooding in South Florida

The Problem

During King Tide, the Atlantic Ocean rises to street level in Miami, Fort Lauderdale, and across South Florida. Saltwater floods back through existing storm drains—destroying infrastructure, killing vegetation, and costing millions. Miami's gravity-based stormwater system (100+ years old) fails when tidal elevation exceeds drainage capacity. Existing passive check valves cannot prevent backflow during compound flood events (rain + high tide).

  • Finding 1: Miami's gravity drain system was inadequate before sea-level rise. Certain low-elevation areas could not drain naturally, redirecting water back into the system.
  • Finding 2: Backflow prevention valves are proven effective. Southern Underground Industries installed WaStop check valves in Miami Beach's stormwater system—the 2014 King Tide test kept South Beach streets dry (validated by ScienceDirect research).
  • Finding 3: Passive valves have fatal flaws. Duckbill check valves cannot prevent compound floods and can be held open by debris or biological growth if not regularly cleaned (documented in Charleston, SC case studies).
  • Finding 4: Miami's 2012 pump program failed due to underpowered design. Pumps did not account for local sea-level rise projections and were insufficient from installation (Wapro analysis).

The Solution: Sentry

A retrofittable smart valve that installs inside existing stormwater pipes. Unlike passive systems, Sentry actively prevents coastal flooding while maintaining drainage capability.

  • IoT-Connected Brain Box: Receives real-time forecast data (tidal predictions, rainfall warnings, storm surge alerts)
  • Smart Inflation System: Deploys a rubber bladder to fully seal the stormwater pipe when backflow risk is high
  • One-Way Flap Valve: Allows rainwater to drain outward even when ocean pressure is pushing inward
  • Micro-Hydro Turbine Power: Generates electricity from flowing stormwater—zero grid dependency, fully autonomous
  • Network Integration: Multi-unit deployment mapping with scenario-based optimization
  • Mobile Fleet Control: Native iOS/Android app for real-time monitoring, remote activation, and historical flood event logs
